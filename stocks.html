Understanding Stocks

Stocks represent ownership in a company. When you buy a stock, you purchase a small piece of that company, which means you have a claim on its assets and earnings. Stocks are bought and sold on stock exchanges, like the Bombay Stock Exchange (BSE) and the National Stock Exchange (NSE) in India.

Key Points

  • Ownership: Buying stocks means you own a part of the company. If the company does well, your stocks may increase in value.
  • Dividends: Some companies share their profits with shareholders by paying dividends, which are regular cash payments made to stockholders.
  • Market Value: The price of stocks fluctuates based on supply and demand in the market.
  • Long-term Investment: Many investors buy stocks expecting the company to grow over time.
  • Risk: Investing in stocks comes with risks, as stock prices can be volatile.
  • Stock Market: The stock market is where stocks are bought and sold through brokers or online platforms.

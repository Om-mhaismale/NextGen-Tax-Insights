1. Income Sources Classification

Active income includes:

  • Salary: Basic pay, allowances (like HRA, DA), bonuses, commissions, and other benefits received by employees.
  • Income from Business or Profession: Profits from business activities, self-employment, freelancing, and other professional services.

2. Taxable Income Calculation

Gross Income: Calculate total earnings from active income sources.

Allowable Deductions: Subtract eligible deductions (e.g., Standard Deduction, Provident Fund contributions, HRA exemption) to arrive at the taxable amount.

3. Salary Components and Exemptions

  • Standard Deduction: Employees are eligible for a standard deduction of ₹50,000 from their gross salary.
  • House Rent Allowance (HRA): Deduction for HRA is available if the taxpayer lives in rented accommodation and meets certain conditions.
  • Professional Tax: Some states levy professional tax, which can be deducted from gross salary.
  • Other Allowances and Perquisites: Certain allowances (like leave travel allowance) and perquisites may be exempt or partially exempt from tax.

4. Income Tax Slabs for FY 2024-2025

Active income is taxed based on the following slabs:

  • Up to ₹3,00,000: Nil
  • ₹3,00,001 to ₹7,00,000: 5%
  • ₹7,00,001 to ₹10,00,000: 10%
  • ₹10,00,001 to ₹12,00,000: 15%
  • ₹12,00,001 to ₹15,00,000: 20%
  • Above ₹15,00,000: 30%

5. Income from Business and Profession

Business income includes profits or gains derived from business or professional services.

Allowable Expenses: Expenses directly related to the business (like rent, utilities, employee salaries) can be deducted from gross income.

Presumptive Taxation: Small businesses and professionals with turnover under certain limits can opt for presumptive taxation (Sections 44AD, 44ADA), simplifying tax calculations.
